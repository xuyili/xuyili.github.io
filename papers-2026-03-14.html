To Words and Beyond: Probing Large Language Models for Sentence-Level Psycholinguistic Norms of Memorability and Reading Times

➡️ 从词语到超越：探究大语言模型在句子层面的心理语言学规范
👤 多个作者 | 最近更新
📄 Large Language Models (LLMs) have recently been shown to produce estimates of psycholinguistic norms, such as valence, arousal, or concreteness, for words and multiword expressions, that correlate with human judgments. These estimates are obtained by prompting an LLM, in zero-shot fashion, with a question similar to those used in human studies. Meanwhile, for other norms such as lexical decision time or age of acquisition, LLMs require supervised fine-tuning to obtain results that align with ground-truth values. In this paper, we extend this approach to the previously unstudied features of sentence memorability and reading times.
📝 大语言模型（LLM）最近被证明可以产生心理语言学规范的估计，如词汇和短语的情绪、唤醒度或具体性，与人类判断相关。这些估计是通过以零样本方式提示LLM，使用与人类研究中相似的问题获得的。同时，对于其他规范如词汇决策时间或习得年龄，LLM需要监督微调才能获得与真实值一致的结果。在本文中，我们将这种方法扩展到之前未研究的句子记忆性和阅读时间特征。

👤 多个作者 | 最近更新

📝 随着大语言模型的快速发展，系统评估其多语言和多文化能力变得越来越重要。之前的文化评估基准主要集中在可以以语言形式编码的基本文化知识。在这里，我们提出了SommBench，一个多语言基准来评估侍酒师专业知识，这是一个根植于嗅觉和味觉的领域。

Cross-Context Review: Improving LLM Output Quality by Separating Production and Review Sessions

➡️ 跨上下文审查：通过分离生成和审查会话来提高LLM输出质量
👤 多个作者 | 最近更新
📄 Large language models struggle to catch errors in their own outputs when the review happens in the same session that produced them. This paper introduces Cross-Context Review (CCR), a straightforward method where the review is conducted in a fresh session with no access to the production conversation history.
📝 当审查发生在生成输出的同一会话中时，大语言模型难以发现自身输出中的错误。本文介绍了跨上下文审查（CCR），这是一种简单的方法，在没有生成对话历史的新会话中进行审查。
